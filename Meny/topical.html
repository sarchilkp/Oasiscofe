The interplay between technology and society


Foto: Pixabay

Technology and social values are strongly connected. Before the time of technology, humans have been building tools and cultures to improve our life’s and this has not changed in the world of data. It is a fact that technology have made daily tasks easier, such as the washing machines, as the invention made room and time in the individualistic life where one can spend time on other things. Our field of study will look at the digital practices implemented at school, and how it has affected its society.

Foto: pixabay


The school system has been highly affected by the technological wave. This can be observed just by looking at the investment in computer devices like smartboard, ipads, computers etc. Research has shown that we learn better by doing, creating and watching than just listening and reading. School system now have to change the way of learning and the minds of the new generation. Innovation will be a benefitting skill as we tend to not stay for the same amount of time in our jobs as earlier. Our social values are affected by this because we now expect more flexibility in learning methods and the availability of information at any time, any subject and anywhere in the world. Making it no longer limited who can learn if the access to internet is available.
We concluded that we approve of this change as each student now can learn in new ways, as it is giving us the opportunity to document and analyze how and what each student’s strength and weakness is by using algorithms. The downside of this is the unknow long lasting effects and the difference in each school ́s ability to follow this trend, giving some students an upper hand. As well as the concerns to changing habits for old teachers [1].

The internet has a key role in creating different channels of communication that among other things circulates news. Especially through social media. With easy access to any information from any corner of the world, the internet has reformed the world with its power to change not only societal values, but also dynamics of conflict and political issues. For example, in a report from Pew Research Centre, citizens from countries such as Jordan, Tunisia and Lebanon indicated that access to internet and social media had made it easier to be informed about current events in the political environment, making it easier to be involved. Alternatively, the same report shows charts of negative affiliation to the internet as many believe it is easier to be manipulated towards the government’s interests. Many Jordanians stated that a visualisation of opinion through surveys and charts given by the government was one of the easiest ways to sway the public towards the majority’s opinion [2] . An article from CSIS shows that countries such as China, Russia, Iran and other authoritarian countries recognize the political risk that comes with the internet but rather use the platform for manipulating opinions and knowledge of their own populations. Russia is a known benefactor of cognitive manipulation whereas they use cyber-attacks to spread information that changes thoughts and behaviour by taking advantage of human pride, instincts and feel for nationalism [3]. As a result of such authoritarian tendencies and exploitation of the public, an annual review from Economics shows that there is an increasing wave of protests, revolutions and movements, made to countermeasure notable control and manipulation inflicted on the public [4]. To conclude we can both see positive and negative political consequences, which all have impactful affects.
